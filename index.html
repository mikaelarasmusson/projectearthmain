WHAT IS PROJECT EARTH?

We are a team of 16 young adults studying at Malmö University and together we decided to create a website to show other adults that it’s very important to not just talk about climate change as a thing of the future, but also of the present. Our mission was founded during a group project in 2023 where we realized how fundamental it is to spark people's actual interest in making a difference.

1.5°C DEGREES OR ELSE..?

The 1.5-degree goal, enshrined in the Paris Agreement, represents a critical threshold for global climate action. It signifies the aspiration to limit the rise in the Earth's average temperature to 1.5 degrees Celsius above pre-industrial levels.Achieving the 1.5-degree goal is imperative for safeguarding our ecosystems, communities, and future generations. It is a recognition that even a half-degree increase in temperature has profound consequences, from intensified heatwaves and sea-level rise to more frequent and severe extreme weather events.

Extreme heat

Earth's Climate Crisis: Extreme heat brings catastrophic wildfires and heatwaves, reflecting our collective choices, pushing us toward an existential nightmare. We need to take action to reduce greenhouse gas emissions and build a more resilient world. This means making changes to our lifestyles and our economies.

Pollution

Pollution, in its many insidious forms, casts a looming shadow over our environment and health. Join us as we dicsuss the hidden impacts of pollution. The hidden impacts of pollution are often the most insidious.

Flooding

Flooding, a global problem exacerbated by climate change, is devastating communities and threatening lives. With far-reaching consequences, including crop loss, livestock deaths, and water contamination, flooding demands urgent action. We must reduce extreme weather events and restore natural flood defenses to build a more resilient world.

... BUT IT IS NOT TOO LATE-YET!

Maine’ Puffins are recovering

After a rough 2021, the species had a second successful rebound year for fledgling chicks. The sea birds’ resilience and ability to reproduce, despite increasing threats due to climate change, is a glimmer of hope for scientists.

Coral reefs are adapting!

Scientists have researched the reef system in Palau, an island country in the western pacific ocean, and found that increases in the heat tolerance of reefs is possible and can keep pace with a warming ocean.
